# Historical Reference: The Chronicles

## 1. Chronicon Terrae Prussiae (Peter von Dusburg)
*   **Author**: A Teutonic priest.
*   **Tone**: Pure propaganda, but rich in detail. 
*   **Key Content**: Describes the "barbaric" rites of the Prussians and Lithuanians. He is the one who popularized the idea of the "Kriwe-Kriwajto" (Pagan Pope).

## 2. The Livonian Rhymed Chronicle
*   **Period**: 1180–1343.
*   **Key Content**: Focuses on the northern Crusades. It describes the bravery of Lithuanian warriors and the specific tactics of the "Light Baltic Cavalry."

## 3. Die Littauischen Wegeberichte
*   **Context**: A Teutonic military guide to the Lithuanian forests/swamps.
*   **Story Value**: Provides literal "maps" of where the castles and hidden groves were. Excellent for planning "escape and flight" chapters.

## 4. The chronicles of Jan Długosz
*   **Perspective**: Polish, 15th century.
*   **Key Content**: Provides the best accounts of the Battle of Grunwald and the personal lives of Jogaila and Jadwiga.
