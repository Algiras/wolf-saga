# Historical Reference: Daily Life & Symbols

## 1. Diet (The Food of the North)
*   **Staples**: Rye bread (sacred), barley, beets, turnips, mushrooms (over 400 varieties).
*   **Meat**: Wild boar, venison, beaver (for the wealthy), pike, herring (marinated/aspic).
*   **Drink**: Mead (honey-wine), beer.
*   **Luxury**: Honey, imported spices from the East (Byzantine influence), cucumbers, cherries.

## 2. Clothing & Rank
*   **Materials**: Linen, wool, imported silk/brocade (for sashes/headdresses).
*   **Women**: Woolen checked skirts, bodices of damask/velvet, crowns of coiled brass for the high-born.
*   **Men**: Coarse grey/brown wool overcoats (rudinė), fur-lined żupans for the nobility.
*   **Jewelry**: Heavy brass/silver bracelets, shell necklaces, amber amulets (cone-shaped).

## 3. Baltic Symbols (The Language of the Soul)
*   **The World Tree**: Roots (underworld), Trunk (earth), Branches (sky).
*   **The Thunder Cross (Pērkonkrusts)**: Swastika-shape. Fire, lightning, luck, and protection.
*   **The Grass Snake**: Fertility and the life cycle.
*   **The Little Horses**: Divine protection for the home.
*   **Columns of Gediminas**: Official symbol of the dynasty; represents the Trakai gates.
*   **Vytis (The Chaser)**: Armored knight on horseback (adopted early 15th century).
